Motion in a Straight Line

Key Concepts in Motion

Motion

An object is said to be in motion if its position changes with respect to time and a reference point.

Rest

An object is said to be at rest if its position does not change with respect to time and a reference point.

Distance

The total length of the path traveled by an object, irrespective of direction.

Formula: \( d = s_1 + s_2 + ... + s_n \)

Displacement

The shortest straight-line distance between the initial and final position of an object.

Formula: \( s = x_f - x_i \)

Velocity

The rate of change of displacement with respect to time.

Formula (Average Velocity): \( v = \frac{s}{t} \)

Formula (Instantaneous Velocity): \( v = \frac{ds}{dt} \)

Acceleration

The rate of change of velocity with respect to time.

Formula (Average Acceleration): \( a = \frac{v_f - v_i}{t} \)

Formula (Instantaneous Acceleration): \( a = \frac{dv}{dt} \)

Uniform Motion

An object is in uniform motion if it covers equal distances in equal intervals of time.

Formula: \( v = \frac{s}{t} \), where velocity remains constant.

Non-Uniform Motion

An object is in non-uniform motion if it covers unequal distances in equal intervals of time.

Formula: \( v_{\text{avg}} = \frac{\Delta s}{\Delta t} \)

Average Velocity

Average velocity is the total displacement divided by the total time taken.

Formula:

\[ v_{\text{avg}} = \frac{s_{\text{total}}}{t_{\text{total}}} = \frac{x_f - x_i}{t_f - t_i} \]

Example 1:

A car moves from \( x = 2 \) m to \( x = 10 \) m in 4 seconds. Find its average velocity.

Solution:

\[ v_{\text{avg}} = \frac{10 - 2}{4} = \frac{8}{4} = 2 \text{ m/s} \]

Example 2:

A person walks 5 m east and then 12 m west in 6 seconds. Find their average velocity.

Solution:

Displacement = \( 12 - 5 = 7 \) m (west)

\[ v_{\text{avg}} = \frac{-7}{6} \approx -1.17 \text{ m/s} \]

The negative sign indicates motion towards the west.

Average Speed

Average speed is the total distance traveled divided by the total time taken.

General Formula:

\[ \text{Average Speed} = \frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Total Time}} \]

Case 1: When distances \( s_1, s_2, s_3, \dots \) are given with respective speeds \( v_1, v_2, v_3, \dots \)

\[ v_{\text{avg}} = \frac{s_1 + s_2 + s_3 + \dots}{\frac{s_1}{v_1} + \frac{s_2}{v_2} + \frac{s_3}{v_3} + \dots} \]

Case 2: When a particle moves at different speeds for equal time intervals \( t_1, t_2, t_3, \dots \)

\[ v_{\text{avg}} = \frac{v_1 + v_2 + v_3 + \dots}{n} \]

where \( n \) is the number of time intervals.

Case 3: When a particle travels equal distances at different speeds

\[ v_{\text{avg}} = \frac{2 v_1 v_2}{v_1 + v_2} \]

Equations of Uniformly Accelerated Motion

When an object moves with uniform acceleration, the following three equations describe its motion:

First Equation of Motion:

\[ v = u + at \]

where:

  • \( v \) = Final velocity
  • \( u \) = Initial velocity
  • \( a \) = Acceleration
  • \( t \) = Time
Second Equation of Motion:

\[ s = ut + \frac{1}{2} a t^2 \]

where:

  • \( s \) = Displacement
Third Equation of Motion:

\[ v^2 = u^2 + 2as \]

Example:

Problem: A car starts from rest and accelerates at \( 2 \, m/s^2 \) for 5 seconds. Find its final velocity and displacement.

Solution:

  • Given: \( u = 0 \), \( a = 2 \, m/s^2 \), \( t = 5 \) sec
  • Using first equation: \( v = 0 + (2 \times 5) = 10 \, m/s \)
  • Using second equation: \( s = (0 \times 5) + \frac{1}{2} (2 \times 5^2) \)
  • \( s = 0 + \frac{1}{2} (2 \times 25) = 25 \) m

Answer: Final velocity = \( 10 \) m/s, Displacement = \( 25 \) m

Motion Under Gravity

When an object moves under the influence of gravity alone, it experiences uniform acceleration due to gravity (\( g \)). The equations of motion remain the same, but acceleration (\( a \)) is replaced by (\( g \)).

Equations of Motion Under Gravity:
  • \( v = u + gt \) (For objects in free fall, take \( g = +9.8 \) m/s² downward)
  • \( s = ut + \frac{1}{2} g t^2 \)
  • \( v^2 = u^2 + 2gs \)
For Objects Thrown Upwards:

When an object is thrown upwards, acceleration due to gravity is taken as negative (\( g = -9.8 \) m/s²).

  • \( v = u - gt \)
  • \( s = ut - \frac{1}{2} g t^2 \)
  • \( v^2 = u^2 - 2gs \)
Important Concepts:
  • At the highest point, velocity \( v = 0 \).
  • Time to reach max height: \( t = \frac{u}{g} \)
  • Max height reached: \( h = \frac{u^2}{2g} \)
  • Total time of flight (up + down): \( T = \frac{2u}{g} \)
Example:

Problem: A ball is thrown upwards with a velocity of \( 20 \) m/s. Find:

  • Time to reach max height
  • Maximum height
  • Total time of flight

Solution:

  • Given: \( u = 20 \) m/s, \( g = 9.8 \) m/s²
  • Time to reach max height: \( t = \frac{u}{g} = \frac{20}{9.8} \approx 2.04 \) s
  • Max height: \( h = \frac{u^2}{2g} = \frac{20^2}{2 \times 9.8} \approx 20.4 \) m
  • Total time of flight: \( T = \frac{2u}{g} = \frac{2 \times 20}{9.8} \approx 4.08 \) s
